How Energy Is Estimated

EcoPrompt uses a two-component model: frontend device cost (measured) plus backend AI inference cost (estimated from published research).

💻
Frontend Heuristic
DOM mutations, network bytes, and media activity are sampled every 10 s. The result is a device-side power draw in the 0.1–5 W range, scaled by observed activity level.
±30% vs measured browser idle/active power
🤖
AI Backend Inference
Query count is estimated at 1 query per 3 min of session time, capped at 20. Each query's energy is looked up from a database of published values, then amortized over a 30 s window.
±50% vs per-query metered measurements

Model Energy Database

Per-query energy values (Wh) are drawn from third-party research and averaged across hardware configurations. Actual inference energy varies with prompt length, batch size, and datacenter efficiency.

EcoPrompt
Version 0.1.0
Minimal prompt optimizer and energy tracker for AI tools.
What it does
  • Tracks browser energy use in real time across all tabs
  • Detects AI platforms and adds backend inference cost
  • Optimizes prompts to reduce token count and energy
  • Compares your AI usage to real-world equivalents
Privacy
  • All data stored locally via chrome.storage.local
  • No personal data leaves your browser
  • Prompt optimization runs entirely client-side
  • History is capped at 120 entries (~20 min)
Architecture
Content Script
Collects DOM, network & media metrics every 10 s
→
Service Worker
Estimates watts, detects AI platform, writes history
→
Popup / Options
Reads pre-computed watts from storage for display
Built with no external dependencies — pure Manifest V3
